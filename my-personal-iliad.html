My personal Iliad.

Publicado em: Mar 30th 2026

Why the Dodgers? Why The Iliad?

Well, that’s a great question, and the answer is quite simple: why not the Dodgers? Why not The Iliad?

I’m a huge baseball fan and a history student. These are two of my greatest passions! When I thought about creating my own website, I wanted it to express the things I love. Even though I can’t translate everything I cherish into lines of code (God help me if I ever tried), I thought it would be a beautiful mixture of two epic things that define my daily life: baseball and history.

My history with the LA Dodgers is quite an odd one. For starters, I’m not from the USA, and obviously, I’m not from L.A. I’m from Brazil, a country where most people don't give a damn about this sport. In fact, most don’t even know how the game works or which teams play it. Sure, you can spot the occasional Dodgers or Yankees cap in a crowd, but they’re worn as fashion accessories rather than fan items.

When I was around 15, I was bored at home with nothing to do. My Spurs weren't playing that day, and I was desperate for something to watch. I tuned into ESPN and there he was: Walker Buehler on the mound.

I thought that man was magic! What he could do with that little ball—the sheer speed of his pitches—was insane. I was hooked from the start; Buehler gained a fan for life. You might think "he wasn't all that" back then, but to me, he was fantastic. It’s because of him (and later, Kershaw) that I fell in love with this game. But it wasn't just the pitchers. I watched Bellinger win the MVP in an amazing campaign that made me cry for joy. I saw Kiké Hernandez hit home runs that had me jumping in the middle of my living room. I watched Max Muncy lead the stats. It was love at first sight. (You can imagine how much it hurt when Buehler and Bellinger eventually left the team).

Since then, the Dodgers have played a huge role in my life. Unfortunately, I can’t watch every game because of the time difference and the responsibilities of adult life, but whenever I can, I put on my jersey and cheer.

This bond only grew stronger during the COVID-19 pandemic. I missed the team so much it actually hurt—they are my second family, after all. Despite the conditions, the Dodgers had an impressive 2020 season. Those playoffs against the Braves had me on edge. I’m not a naturally optimistic person, so I felt defeated after we went down 0-2. But Game 3 gave me life again! We won 15-3, and I thought: WE ARE SO BACK.

The World Series against the Rays was madness. Watching both Buehler and Kershaw get their wins made me incredibly happy. Then came Game 6. Victor González was pitching, and I was nervous. He was good, but my confidence was shaking. Thank the heavens, that Mexican lad proved me wrong! I couldn't even sleep that night. It was the first time I witnessed the Dodgers win a World Series. It was truly special.

However, the 2021 season was a dark period. I was going through difficult personal stuff and didn't have the energy for the game. I left the Dodgers behind for a bit. There was something in me that was suffering, and I couldn't handle the long, slow pace of baseball.

2022 and 2023 were more of the same. A new job brought me close to burnout. I’d wake up early, get home late, and naturally, I forgot about the diamond. But you can't truly quit this sport, can you? In 2024, life improved. I found a job that allowed me to breathe again while recovering from burnout and fighting depression. My doctor told me to find something that used to bring me joy. I thought for days: what was it?

The universe has a funny way of presenting solutions. A few days later, a tweet about Shohei Ohtani appeared on my timeline. Just like that, I was hooked again. I fell back into the Dodgers loophole and never left. Life still gets in the way sometimes, but the Dodgers are like that one friend who is always there when you need them most, no matter how ugly things get.

So, why "The Iliad"?

Just like the Dodgers, history is my soul. I’ve read thousands of books and started my degree in the field. The name The Iliad resonates with my current journey.

The major themes of Homer’s epic—revenge, war, mortality, love, fate, and honor—reflect the human condition. Achilles demonstrates that while we cannot escape our fate, we must accept it with honor. Even the gods cannot change destiny.

The Iliad carries a message that has stuck with me since grad school: while we are alive, we have the opportunity to write stories that will be told long after we are gone. This website is my personal Iliad. It’s a place to celebrate the person I am becoming in this beautiful, flawed, and complicated life.

Just like Achilles, I embrace my path. I make mistakes, and I’m not always the greatest person, but I am learning to make peace with that. I must honor who I am. As Clarice Lispector once said: “Já que sou, o jeito é ser.” (Since I am, the only way is to be).

Thank you for reading.
