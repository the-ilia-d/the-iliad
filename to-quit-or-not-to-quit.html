To quit, or not to quit, that is the question.

Once again, talking about the “quitting my smartphone” subject: today, I was scrolling through the r/dumbphone subreddit and found a post where a lady around my age shared her impressions on 180 days without a smartphone. She bought the Barbie flip phone a while back and started using it as her primary device, totally quitting that vicious gadget that we (for some reason) have become used to carrying in our pockets at all times.

Anyway, that post opened my eyes a bit more. I’ve been feeling like quitting this thing for as long as I can remember, yet I feel like I never take concrete actions to actually make that leap. And I wondered: why not?

When I first thought about quitting my phone, it was around 2019/2020. At the time, I had a girlfriend who wasn’t too keen on helping me with that. She didn’t understand my reasons and told me she wouldn’t waste money on data just so she could SMS me. It left me a bit sad, but I understood her perspective—or at least, that’s what I told myself. After all, we have WhatsApp, Telegram, and many other messaging apps that make it way easier and more conventional than SMS. So, I put that idea aside for a while. We eventually broke up and I felt like trying again, but then an unfortunate job came into my life, and I needed to have a stupid smartphone with me during my 10-hour shift. As you can imagine, there was no quitting my smartphone back then.

After that, I kind of gave up on the idea. I thought it was impossible to navigate this world without a smartphone because I had too much to lose.

Recently, I’ve been feeling this way again (a stupid feeling, I know, but you can’t always rationalize your addictions), and after reading that post, I was left wondering: why am I afraid of this? What do I really have to lose by quitting my smartphone? And what does “losing” even mean in this context?

I realized that what I have to lose is convenience: WhatsApp, Google Maps, social media, etc. (everything that actually makes me want to quit). So, “losing” in this context is actually something good for me!

I don’t have a job that requires me to be glued to my phone 24/7, and even if I did, I just got a “work phone” that only has WhatsApp. And then you might think, “What about banking apps?” I carry my credit cards with me, so I don’t need my phone to pay for anything. And since I don’t really have money in my account, no one can rob me (lmao), so that’s not a problem. And if (big IF) a problem does arise, I’m sure it can wait until I get home.

I don’t travel far enough to need Google Maps on the go; I know most places I visit as well as the back of my hand. And if a situation arises where I need to go somewhere new, I can get the instructions on my home or work computer. OR (worst-case scenario—ooh, so spooky!) I can just ask someone for directions!

Other than my girlfriend, family, and a few coworkers, no one actually messages me. When they do, it’s nothing urgent that can’t be fixed with a call—or, in my coworkers’ case, by them coming into my room. So, that’s not a problem either.

I won’t be able to read whatever my heart desires at any moment I want? True, but I do have a Kindle filled with books just waiting for me. What if I can’t read a random fanfic or a comic? Go read Clarice, for Christ’s sake! Besides, I can read fanfiction on a computer!

Anyway, I could keep going with this list, but the point is: the pros clearly outweigh the cons here. So, I need to man up and take a leap of faith. Maybe I’ll feel muuuuch better!

We shall see!
