DREAM BIG

So, I just had the opportunity to watch Marty Supreme (2025), and I must say, I feel like something inside me just changed. It feels like a switch turned on.

As I mentioned in another post, I consider myself a very determined person, and I stand by that. I mean, come on, look at the cool website I created while knowing next to nothing about coding!

Anyway, I watched the movie and I was speechless most of the time by how ambitious Marty is. There’s a scene I liked quite a lot where Marty is talking to Kay and she asks, “What if everything goes wrong?” to which he replies: “That doesn’t even enter my consciousness.” Honestly, I felt that.

I’ve been trying to be more positive about life as a whole, but mainly, I’m trying to trust myself more and believe completely in my potential and abilities. Although I’m obviously not good at table tennis, I am good at other things.

Even though Marty is a prick and a very flawed character, I think there’s a lot to learn from him—not just his obsession with being the greatest, but the belief he has in himself. Sure, sometimes his confidence gets too big for his own good and he becomes too cocky, but in a world that tries to bring us down at every moment, I think one must believe in themselves. Unfortunately, that is often something I lack. Marty doesn’t know where to stop; I don’t know how to start.

I’m not really a religious person, and I don’t think there’s a God per se looking out for me; I think if He does exist, He certainly has better things to worry about. But I do believe in fortune, and I believe that it favors the bold. So perhaps I need to become bolder.

I used to be much more confident when I was younger, but as I’ve grown older and came out as trans, life has become more complicated. Work feels riskier—the fear of losing a job and not being hired elsewhere is real. Family sometimes turns their back, and you’re constantly fighting for basic respect. Even a doctor’s visit becomes an exercise in enduring someone else’s discomfort. The struggle seems endless, and there’s a lot at risk right now. But I still feel like where I am is not where I’m meant to be. I am too big for this “jail”—there are things I want to accomplish that I’ll never reach if I stay in this city, or in a body that feels like a cage. I need a way out. I need to DREAM BIG, and if I learned anything from this movie, it’s that no dream is too big to do everything in your power to reach it.

Let’s be serious: Marty almost got killed, he was humiliated by a rich guy, and he was basically homeless—yet he didn’t give up, even though all the odds were against him. Sure, he was a jerk to the people around him, especially his friends and girlfriend, but that man certainly knows how to believe in himself.

There’s a lot to learn from Marty Supreme (not being that cocky is surely one of those lessons), but the main takeaway is: Trust yourself. Dream big.

After all, fortune favors the bold.

Thank you for reading.

Until next time.
