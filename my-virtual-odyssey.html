My Virtual Odyssey

For a while now, I’ve wanted to quit social media, hide my smartphone from myself, and become a full-time dumbphone user. But, as we know, that’s always easier said than done, right?

A few days ago, I watched a video by a guy whose name I can’t recall, where he decided to take a leap of faith and fulfill this “prophecy.” He ditched his phone, deleted social media, and tried to live life that way.
He failed, obviously.

Social media and smartphones are an addiction, just like cigarettes — maybe worse. In order to fully quit, we have to take baby steps. After failing, he realized he needed a new plan, one that was much healthier but still kept him connected to the World Wide Web. So, he created a website.

That website became an extension of him; everything he would have posted on social media, he posted there instead. In a few days, his personal website was filled with what it meant to be him. It was beautiful. He later added a guestbook where visitors could leave a few words. Most told him he inspired them to take a leap of faith too, though perhaps without their own website. After all, not everyone knows how to code.

I was one of those inspired people. But unlike most of my peers, even though I didn’t know how to code (I’m a complete noob), I decided I wanted to learn. I’m a very determined person, so when I set my mind to something, you better believe I’ll accomplish it.

I started watching HTML tutorials; it wasn’t hard, but it wasn’t easy either. Then I got into CSS, and things got more complicated. Unfortunately, I hit a roadblock. Problems kept showing up and I didn’t know what to do. So, I did what any person who has tried everything would do in this day and age… though I’m not proud of it: I asked an AI for help.

With Gemini’s help, my ideas started to take shape (a beautiful shape, by the way) and I started to understand the process better. In the meantime, I learned about a tool called Publii. It seemed like the solution to all my problems. I’d be able to sleep well at night again!

As lucky as I am, Publii was a PAIN IN THE ASS. Goddamn it, what a boring app. Sure, things would go smoothly if I had used one of their templates, but guess what? They’re BORING! I already had my own beautiful template, made with my own blood, sweat, and tears. I didn’t want to use theirs!

So, I tried to create my own. At first, it seemed like things would go smoothly, but every time I thought “I AM DONE,” Publii would say: “Hell no, you’re not. I just broke this!” and I’d spend another hour trying to understand what I did wrong.

Eventually, I was able to “upload” my code to a website. I used Neocities, by the way. Unfortunately, Neocities and Publii decided they wouldn’t get along, so things broke all the damn time. After wanting to kill myself in front of my computer, I decided to quit Neocities and try a server that works directly with Publii. I went to Netlify. I thought: “This is the one for me!”

Everything seemed great at first. The uploads worked perfectly, the site looked good, etc. I was happy. But then, Netlify decided to pause my website… FOR NO REASON, MIND YOU! And I thought: “There’s absolutely no way in hell I’m paying to host what’s supposed to be a hobby. I’M BROKE, FUCKERS!”

Just when I was about to give up, Gemini told me: “Use GitHub.” Just like that, I revived like a phoenix. I bounced back. Big Sean would be so proud of me.

Thank you, Linus Torvalds, for creating Git; it’s an amazing tool. And thanks to whoever created GitHub and GitHub Pages. I did all the steps, my code was there, and everything was finally going smoothly. I had to change some lines of code because of the Publii and Netlify mess, but eventually, everything was ready.

It was time to see if it would work…

IT DID!

Not everything was perfect at first, but I fixed a bunch of stuff and, just like dominoes, every piece fell into place. Now, just like Odysseus, I have finally finished my journey (or at least part of it), and I must return home.

Thank you for reading.
Until next time!
